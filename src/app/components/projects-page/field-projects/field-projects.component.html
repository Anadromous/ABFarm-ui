Like the house, the pasture and wooded area had not been kept up for many years. Acres of blackberries covered the back area, and the pastures were weed ridden with Thistle and Queen Anne's lace. But fencing became our top priority. So that's what we did first.

Fencing

It's hard to raise animals when you don't have a perimeter fence to keep them in - and predators out. It was the middle of winter by the time we got to fencing, and not the ideal time to be putting up a fence. But we planned to get our first flock of sheep, some goats and a horse that spring, so despite the rain and muddy terrain, we set out to get our fence built.

Our first step was the design. You wouldn't think that an essentially rectangular fence would take much to figure out, but we had property lines to confirm, large trees to avoid, gate placement, and integration with our barn. Plus we wanting it to be asthetically pleasing.
Putting in fence posts is just hard work, and stretching the fencing takes some practice. We couldn't have gotten our fence put in without the help of our fence heros, Arturo and his sons, who showed us how it is done right. So we learned from him, and have since installed additional cross fencing and repaired multiple stretches from tree damage.
There is not really a lot more to say about fences. But did I mention it is really hard work?
